Notes from the 7th Power & Gas Forum: from capacity growth to constraint management

After the 7th Power & Gas Forum in Athens (organized by energypress.gr), one idea stood out clearly: the core challenge is no longer adding capacity, but managing constraints. I attended with LDK Consultants and colleagues Dr. Marios Stanitsas, Konstantinos Koumkoudis, and Thanos Vazakas.

Main takeaways:

  • Grid development must become the central priority. It remains the bottleneck for RES growth and electrification.
  • Data centres are increasing pressure on networks; clear rules are needed for grid allocation and reservation duration.
  • Smart meters and real-time data are now core infrastructure for market operation and flexibility services.
  • Nuclear is back in policy discussions. Neighboring countries are moving faster, while SMRs are still not commercial and Greece faces non-trivial barriers.
  • Hydrogen remains challenged by high production costs, relatively flat demand growth, and limited efficiency progress.

In short: planning discipline and infrastructure governance now matter as much as generation ambition.
