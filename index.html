Insights from the 7th Power & Gas Forum in Athens

On 6–7 April 2026, I attended the 7th Power & Gas Forum in Athens, organised by energypress at Zeus Wyndham Grand Athens. The forum brought together representatives from political leadership, regulators, operators, utilities, investors, technology providers and academia to discuss new realities in Greek electricity and gas markets.

The energy transition has moved beyond the simple question of how much renewable capacity can be added. The core challenge is now how effectively the system can manage constraints, integrate flexibility, modernise infrastructure and operate under increasingly complex market conditions.

  • Grid development remains a key bottleneck for the next phase of RES growth and electrification.
  • Curtailment is becoming a structural issue that signals the need for storage, demand response, forecasting and network reinforcement.
  • Battery storage (BESS) is now central in market design, requiring strong optimisation, forecasting and multi-market participation strategies.
  • Smart meters, digitalisation and real-time data are foundational for dynamic tariffs and consumer-side flexibility.
  • Data center growth introduces new load and adequacy challenges for planners and system operators.

Overall, Greece’s transition is entering a more mature phase where implementation quality matters most: network planning, queue governance, storage integration, market design and real-time operation.
