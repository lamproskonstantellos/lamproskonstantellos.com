Notes from the 7th Power & Gas Forum: from capacity growth to constraint management

After the 7th Power & Gas Forum in Athens, one message stood out: the key issue is no longer only adding capacity, but managing constraints with operational discipline and long-horizon planning.

  • Grid development remains the critical bottleneck for renewable growth and electrification.
  • Data center demand is increasing pressure on network allocation and queue governance.
  • Smart meters and real-time data are now core infrastructure for market and flexibility services.
  • Nuclear is returning to policy dialogue, while deployment barriers remain significant in Greece.
  • Hydrogen still faces high production costs and uncertain near-term demand growth.

The transition conversation is shifting from ambition alone toward implementation quality, infrastructure sequencing, and governance capability.
